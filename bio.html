MAC MILLER

MUSICAN / SONGWRITTER / RECORD PRODUCER

Malcolm James McCormick, professionally known as Mac Miller, was born January 19, 1992 and passed away September 7, 2018 at the age of 27. He was a rapper, singer, songerwritter, and record producer from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. A self-taught musician, Miller played piano, guitar, drums, and bass by the age of six. He first started rapping at the age of fourteen. Miller flew solo with the mixtapes "But My Mackin Ain't Easy", "The Jukebox: Prelude to Class Clown" and "The High Life". Originally went by Easy mac in 2007 on his debut mixtape. In the year 2010, Mac Miller entered into a recording deal with the independent recording label ‘Rostrum Records’. In 2010 his first album ‘Blue Slide Park’ debuted to number one position on the Billboard 200 chart. In 2013,entered into a distribution agreement with Warner Bros. Records for US$ 10 million and launched his own record label ‘REMember Music’. Apart from rapping, Mac Miller also produced records under the name ‘Larry Fisherman'. He produced the video game Grand Theft Auto V music track. Miller passed away due to a accidental drug overdose. His fun, charismatic, warm, and humble demeanor last on with his music and memories of other artist. Donate to the Mac Miller Fund
